Spaghetti With tomato sauce and meatballs

Ingredients

  • 8 oz spaghetti
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the ground beef, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, salt, and pepper until well combined. Roll the mixture into small meatballs.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the meatballs and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove the meatballs from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Add the onion and garlic to the same skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, and salt and pepper to taste. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  5. Add the cooked meatballs to the tomato sauce and simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes.
  6. Serve the spaghetti topped with the meatball and tomato sauce. Garnish with fresh basil leaves.
  7. Enjoy!
